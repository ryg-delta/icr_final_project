To what extent does the neglect of women's health within healthcare stem from barriers in intercultural communication, and how can addressing these barriers lead to more equitable healthcare outcomes for women?

The neglect of women's health issues within healthcare is influenced by cultural, systemic, and institutional biases. These biases create significant barriers in intercultural communication and trust between women and healthcare providers. To develop an effective framework, all three biases must be addressed holistically, integrating intercultural praxis with targeted strategies so that healthcare providers can foster trust and communication, leading to more equitable outcomes for women.
